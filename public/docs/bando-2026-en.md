# "Maria Golia" International Piano Competition

## Terms and Conditions -- 2026 Edition

The association **Orium APS** (Tax Code 93053580010) organises the "Maria Golia" International Piano Competition, open to pianists of all nationalities. The competition will be held from **1 to 3 May 2026** at the **Castello Ducale di Agliè**, Piazza Castello 2, 10011 Agliè (TO), Italy.

**Artistic Director:** Marina Scalafiotti

---

## 1. Competition structure

The sessions are public and are set up in the following sections:

### Section I -- Youth Categories

The Youth Categories consist of a single session with a programme of the competitor's choice.

| Category | Year of birth       | Max duration | Registration fee |
|----------|---------------------|--------------|------------------|
| Cat. 12  | 2019 or later       | 3 minutes    | €30              |
| Cat. 13  | 2017--2018          | 5 minutes    | €30              |
| Cat. 14  | 2015--2016          | 8 minutes    | €35              |
| Cat. 15  | 2013--2014          | 11 minutes   | €40              |
| Cat. 16  | 2010--2012          | 15 minutes   | €45              |
| Cat. 17  | 2006--2009          | 20 minutes   | €45              |

The maximum duration may only be exceeded in the case of the performance of one single composition.

#### Scoring and prizes -- Youth Categories

The scores, established by the Jury Commission and expressed in percentages, will determine the prizes and diplomas to be assigned as follows:

- at least 95%: 1st Prize Certificate. Among these, the competitor who obtains the highest score will receive the diploma for 1st Absolute Prize. The 1st Absolute Prize is indivisible;
- at least 90%: 2nd Prize Certificate;
- at least 85%: 3rd Prize Certificate;
- at least 75%: Participation Award Certificate.

**Prizes:** Gifts and participation diplomas. Special prizes may be attributed at the Jury's discretion.

### Section II -- Maria Golia International

Open to pianists of all nationalities, no age limit.

The competition is organised into two rounds: the pre-selection and the final.

#### Pre-selection (online)

The pre-selection is carried out via video recording submission. The maximum duration is **18 minutes** and the programme must include:

1. A Prelude and Fugue from the "Well-Tempered Clavier" by J.S. Bach;
2. A virtuosic Study by F. Chopin or F. Liszt;
3. A free-choice piece by the candidate.

#### Final Round

The Final Round will have a maximum duration of **35 minutes** and will consist in the execution of a free programme. No piece from the pre-selection may be presented in the Final Round.

#### Scoring -- Maria Golia International

Admission to the Final Round will depend on the judgement of suitability by the majority of the Jury.

The final ranking will be established according to the votes given by members of the Jury, expressed in percentages.

The First Absolute Prize will be assigned to the competitor who obtains a total score of at least 95%. The First Absolute Prize can be awarded to only one competitor.

The Jury has the right not to assign any prize, should the competitors not meet the requirements stipulated in the competition's rules.

A diploma will be assigned to all non-winning participants of the Final Round.

#### Prizes -- Maria Golia International

The Jury Commission will assign the following prizes:

| Place      | Prize   | Concerts   |
|------------|---------|------------|
| 1st Prize  | €1,000  | 4 concerts |
| 2nd Prize  | €500    | 2 concerts |
| 3rd Prize  | €300    | 1 concert  |

Special prizes and scholarships may be attributed at the Jury's discretion.

**The registration fee for the Maria Golia International section is €70** (pre-selection only; finalists pay no additional fee).

## 2. General rules

1. Competitors must perform their programme from memory.

2. Competitors are allowed to apply for a category intended for a greater age than their own.

3. Competitors must present the sheet music of the compositions performed if the Jury Commission requests it.

4. Jury members who have parental or other close relations with any competitor will not be allowed to judge. Jury members who have had private lessons with any competitor in the two years preceding the competition will not be allowed to judge. If the teaching relationship has been of a public nature, the Jury member must abstain from voting for that competitor.

5. The judgements expressed by the Jury Commission are not disputable, not appealable and definitive.

6. The performances may be recorded and broadcast by the organisers of the competition.

7. The winners of first absolute prizes must perform at the winners' concert to be held on 3 May 2026 at the Castello Ducale di Agliè, executing a programme chosen from the presented pieces by the Jury Commission.

8. Winners will lose their right to receive the obtained prizes and concert engagements should they not participate in the winners' concert.

9. The diplomas for winners of absolute and special prizes will be handed over at the end of the final concert. Other diplomas will be handed over during the proclamation of the winners, after voting. Should that not be possible for justifiable reasons, they will be sent by ordinary mail at the participant's expense.

10. The organisers reserve the right to change parts of these terms and to cancel categories if the number of participants is too low.

11. If any issue is not covered in these terms, the Artistic Director will be the competent person to make a decision, as well as for any interpretative question about these terms.

12. Participation in the competition implies the unconditional agreement of these terms without any reservation.

13. For any eventual controversy, the competent court will be that of Turin (Torino), Italy.

## 3. How to apply

### Required documents

Candidates must submit their application online via **www.mariagolia.com**, attaching the following documents:

1. Copy of a valid identity document or passport;
2. Recent photograph;
3. Complete programme of the competition rounds, with personal information of the competitor and the section/category applied for;
4. Brief curriculum vitae;
5. Receipt of the bank transfer for the registration fee;
6. For the Maria Golia International section: video recording of the pre-selection programme.

### Payment

The registration fee, non-refundable in case of withdrawal or absence, must be paid by bank transfer to:

> **Orium APS**
> IBAN: [TO BE PROVIDED]
> Reference: "Registration fee Maria Golia Competition 2026 -- [Full Name]"

Bank transfer is the only accepted method of payment.

### Deadlines

- **Registration opens:** January 2026
- **Registration deadline:** End of February 2026
- **Video submission (International):** March 2026
- **Finalists announcement:** April 2026
- **Competition:** 1--3 May 2026

The Artistic Director has the right to exceptionally accept applications sent after the deadline.

Incomplete or invalid applications will not be considered.

### Competition schedule

The timetable and schedule of the competition will be communicated to all participants after the registration deadline.

**Youth Categories:** The draw of the first letter of the starting competitor's surname will be done before the start of each section's audition, under the supervision of the Artistic Director or a delegated person.

Competitors must present themselves with a valid identity document and, in the case of minors, accompanied by a parent or teacher.

### Practice pianos

At the start of the competition, a schedule for the use of practice pianos will be announced. The schedule will be planned to guarantee a fair distribution of practice time among all competitors.
